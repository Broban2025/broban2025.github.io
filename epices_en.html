What are these spices contained in your little pouch?

Discover these three treasures of Oriental cuisine that we have selected for you!

Za'atar

Composition: Dried thyme, ground sumac, toasted sesame seeds, oregano, marjoram, and salt.

Aroma: Aromatic and herbaceous with a hint of acidity from the sumac. Za'atar releases fresh notes of thyme and oregano, enhanced by the crunch of sesame seeds.

Uses: Traditionally sprinkled on pita bread with olive oil, za'atar also enhances salads, grilled vegetables, white meats, and fish. It can be mixed with olive oil to create a delicious spread or used as a seasoning for eggs and fresh cheeses.

Sumac

Composition: Dried and ground berries of red sumac (Rhus coriaria), without any additives.

Aroma: Tangy and fruity, sumac reminds of lemon flavor with slightly astringent notes. Its intense burgundy red color announces its pronounced and refreshing taste.

Uses: Essential in Middle Eastern cuisine, sumac enhances salads (especially fattoush), grilled meats and fish, legumes, and rice dishes. It can replace lemon in many preparations and brings a beautiful red color to dishes. Perfect also for seasoning onions or sprinkling on hummus.

Lebanese 7-Spice Blend (Baharat)

Composition: Black pepper, allspice, cinnamon, cloves, nutmeg, ginger, and cardamom. Proportions vary according to families and regions.

Aroma: Warm and complex, this blend offers a rich aromatic bouquet mixing the spicy notes of pepper, the sweetness of cinnamon, the freshness of cardamom, and the power of cloves. An enchanting fragrance that immediately evokes the Orient.

Uses: The ultimate versatile spice in Lebanese cuisine, baharat flavors meats (lamb, beef, poultry), stuffed vegetables (eggplants, zucchini), stews, and braised dishes. It's used in many traditional preparations like keftas, kibbehs, and can be used to season pilaf rice or lentils.
